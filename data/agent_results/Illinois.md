### 2. AUTHORIZATION MODEL
**Type:** **Open Access / Public Portal**

**Confidence Level:** ✓ HIGH

**Details:**
- **No formal registration required** for basic UCC searches - the online search portal is publicly accessible
- **No subscription required** for basic searches
- **Optional account creation** for online filing through the electronic filing portal
- **Advanced access available:** For direct database access, users can obtain a Remote Access Identification Number (RAQF ID) through a contract with the Illinois Secretary of State Office of General Counsel

**Search Access:** Completely open to the public via online portal
**Filing Access:** Open to anyone; can file via online portal (with account), mail, or in-person

---

### 3. REQUIREMENTS

#### A. FORMS REQUIRED
- **UCC-1 Financing Statement** - for initial filings
- **UCC-3 Amendment Form** - for amendments, assignments, continuations, partial releases (only one amendment type per form)
- **UCC-11 Information Request Form** - for obtaining document images and certified copies
- Must use the most recently amended national UCC forms approved by the International Association of Commercial Administrators
- Submit two copies with mail filings (one filed, one returned)

**Confidence Level:** ✓ HIGH

#### B. FEES
**Filing Fees:**
- **UCC-1 Initial Financing Statement:** $20 (electronic) / $40 (paper-based)
- **UCC-3 Amendments/Continuations:** $20 per amendment
- **UCC Termination Statement:** $5 (County Recorder) / $10 (otherwise)

**Search Fees:**
- **Basic online search:** FREE (index search only)
- **UCC-11 Information Request (paper-based):** $10 per name searched
- **Document copies:** $1 per page
- **Document images:** $20 (requires UCC-11 form submission)
- **CD-ROM subscription (monthly):** $250/month (for UCC and Federal Tax Lien images)

**Confidence Level:** ✓ MEDIUM-HIGH (fees confirmed from multiple sources including Illinois Administrative Code Title 14 Part 180, though some variation in sources)

#### C. ELIGIBILITY
- **No restrictions** - open to any individual or business
- No background checks required
- No bonding requirements identified

**Confidence Level:** ✓ HIGH

#### D. DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR FILING
- Complete address for each debtor (required on UCC-1)
- Complete address for each secured party (required on UCC-1)
- Self-addressed stamped envelope (for mail filings)

**Confidence Level:** ✓ HIGH

#### E. BACKGROUND CHECKS
- **Not required**

**Confidence Level:** ✓ HIGH

#### F. TECHNOLOGY REQUIREMENTS

### 4. ACCESS METHODS

**A. Online Portal (Primary Method)**
- **UCC Search Portal:** https://apps.ilsos.gov/uccsearch/
  - Free public access to search UCC and Federal Tax Lien index
  - Search by debtor name (personal/business), keyword, or file number
  - Index information only; images require UCC-11 request
  
- **UCC Electronic Filing Portal:** https://apps.ilsos.gov/UCC/
  - Account creation available for online filing
  - Lower fees for electronic filing ($20 vs $40 for paper)

### KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY

1. **Illinois operates an OPEN ACCESS model** - no registration, subscription, or special authorization needed for basic searches
2. **Electronic filing is encouraged** with lower fees ($20 vs $40)
3. **Search index is FREE and publicly accessible** online
4. **Document images contain sensitive information** (SSN, EIN, account numbers) and require formal UCC-11 request with $20 fee
5. **No barriers to entry** - no background checks, bonding, training, or eligibility requirements
6. **Multiple access methods** available (online, mail, in-person)
7. **UCC filings are valid for 5 years** in Illinois
8. **Illinois statute 810 ILCS 5** (Illinois UCC) and **Illinois Administrative Code Title 14 Part 180** govern UCC filings
