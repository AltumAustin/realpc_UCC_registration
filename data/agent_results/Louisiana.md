## Louisiana UCC Search/Filing Access Requirements - Comprehensive Research Report

### 1. FILING OFFICE

**Primary Agency:** Louisiana Secretary of State - UCC Division

**Unique Filing Structure:** Louisiana has a **decentralized filing system** that differs from all other states. While the Secretary of State manages the central database, **all UCC filings must be submitted to Parish Clerks of Court**, not directly to the Secretary of State.

- **Filing Offices:** Any of the 64 Parish Clerks of Court offices
- **Centralized Database:** Louisiana Secretary of State maintains statewide database
- **Filing Location Flexibility:** You may file a UCC-1 with ANY of the 64 Parish Clerks of Court offices, regardless of debtor location or collateral location
- **Statewide Effectiveness:** Once filed at any parish, the filing is transmitted to the central database and becomes statewide effective

**Confidence Level:** HIGH - This is well-documented and confirmed across multiple official sources.

### 2. AUTHORIZATION MODEL

**Model:** **Tiered Public Access System** with three levels:

#### Level 1: Basic Public Access (Free/Pay-Per-Use)
- Open to public without pre-authorization
- File UCC documents online through geauxBIZ portal
- Search available through Parish Clerks of Court offices
- Pay-per-transaction model

#### Level 2: Direct Access Subscription ($400/year)
- Annual subscription program
- Unlimited database searches
- View filings online after 120-day verification period
- Access to acknowledgment letters
- Available to any business/entity willing to pay subscription fee
- Application required (mail-in form and check)

#### Level 3: Bulk Filing/API Access (Subscription-based)
- API integration using standardized UCC XML schema
- Bulk filing capabilities at reduced rates
- Direct system integration for high-volume filers
- Training and production environments available
- Technical support provided

**Confidence Level:** HIGH - Multiple sources confirm this tiered system.

### LOUISIANA'S UNIQUE CIVIL LAW CONSIDERATIONS

#### Civil Law vs. Common Law Impact on UCC:

1. **Terminology Differences:**
   - Louisiana uses "Chapters" instead of "Articles" for UCC divisions
   - Term "Articles" reserved for Louisiana Civil Code provisions
   - Refers to "movable property" (civil law) vs. "personal property" (common law)

2. **UCC Adoption:**
   - Louisiana adopted most UCC provisions with minimal changes
   - **Exception:** Did NOT adopt UCC Articles 2 and 2A (sales and leases)
   - Maintains civil law tradition for governing sale and lease of goods
   - UCC Chapter 9 (secured transactions) took effect January 1, 1990

3. **Property Classification:**
   - Civil law distinction between movable and immovable property
   - UCC Chapter 9 governs security interests in movable property (corporeal and incorporeal)
   - Immovable property mortgages governed separately under La. R.S. 9:2721

4. **Fixtures Definition:**
   - Louisiana defines fixtures based on Civil Code Articles 463, 465, 466, and 467
   - Component parts of immovables have specific treatment

5. **Security Interest Concepts:**
   - Civil law "pledge" concept (Civil Code Articles 3133-3174) informs court interpretations
   - Purchase Money Security Interest (PMSI) rules include unique Louisiana provision
   - Cross-collateralization does not affect PMSI status (La. Commercial Laws 9-107)

6. **Parish-Based Filing System:**
   - Reflects Louisiana's parish structure (vs. counties in other states)
   - Decentralized filing aligns with civil law recording traditions
   - Centralized database is modern overlay on traditional parish system

**Practical Impact:**
- UCC filing procedures are functionally similar to other states
- Legal interpretation may differ due to civil law framework
- Attorneys must understand both UCC and Louisiana Civil Code
- Parish-based filing is unique but straightforward in practice

**Confidence Level:** MEDIUM-HIGH - Civil law differences are documented; practical impact on filing procedures is minimal.
