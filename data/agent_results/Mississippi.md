## MISSISSIPPI UCC SEARCHER/FILING ACCESS REQUIREMENTS - RESEARCH FINDINGS

---

### 1. FILING OFFICE
**Primary Filing Office:** Mississippi Secretary of State  
**Confidence Level:** ✓✓✓ HIGH

**Details:**
- Mississippi uses a **centralized state filing system** with the Secretary of State as the primary filing officer for UCC documents
- **NOT a county-based system** for general commercial transactions (this is contrary to some states)
- **Exception for Real Estate/Fixtures:** County Chancery Clerk offices handle UCC filings that include real property (fixture filings). For land and real estate transactions, the County Clerk serves as the Custodian of Public Land Records
- **Agricultural Liens:** County codes must be included in farm product filings to identify where products are produced, but filing is still centralized through the Secretary of State

**Legal Authority:** Mississippi Code § 75-9-301 et seq.; 1 Miss. Code R. Title 1, Part 4

---

### 2. AUTHORIZATION MODEL
**Type:** Self-Registration / Subscription-Based  
**Confidence Level:** ✓✓ MEDIUM-HIGH

**Details:**
- **Two-Tier Access System:**
  - **Tier 1 - Public Search (Free):** No authorization required; provides basic creditor and debtor information
  - **Tier 2 - Subscription Service (Paid):** Registration required for detailed searches and document viewing

- **No Pre-Approval Required:** Users can self-register for subscription service without special authorization or licensing
- **Account Setup:** After first login, username becomes the user's email address
- **Bulk Data Access:** Separate subscription service available for bulk downloads of UCC/Corp data at https://www.sos.ms.gov/downUCCCorp/login.aspx

**Note:** No evidence found of restricted access, background checks, or professional licensing requirements for UCC searchers

**Access Requirements:**
- **Free Public Search:**
  - No registration required

  - Limited to basic information (creditor/debtor names)

  - Email address for account creation

**Search Request Requirements (Per 1 Miss. Code R. 4-5.4):**
- Must use official search request form available on Secretary of State website
- Form UCC-11 for formal search requests
- Search requests may be delivered by any means that UCC documents can be delivered
- Detailed requirements set forth in Rule 12.2 (full text not publicly accessible online)

**Filing Requirements:**
- Valid debtor name and address required
- Mississippi uses literal search logic (punctuation and spacing matter)
- Organization names must match official source documents exactly
- Individual names should match driver's license or state ID

### KEY FINDINGS & NOTES

1. **Mississippi is NOT County-Based:** Unlike some states, Mississippi operates a centralized UCC filing system at the state level. Counties only handle fixture filings related to real property.

2. **Dual Access System:** Free basic searches are available to the public, while detailed document viewing requires a paid subscription.

3. **Literal Search Logic:** Mississippi's search system is very literal regarding punctuation, spacing, and capitalization. Searchers should try name variations to avoid missing filings.

4. **Online Filing Preferred:** 75% of filings are done online; strongly encouraged by the Secretary of State due to immediate posting and lower fees.

5. **No Authorization Barriers:** No evidence of restricted access, licensing requirements, or approval processes for becoming a UCC searcher in Mississippi.

6. **Limited Fee Transparency:** While filing fees are publicly listed, subscription service pricing requires direct contact with the office.

7. **Regulatory Framework:** Administrative rules (1 Miss. Code R. Title 1, Part 4) govern UCC procedures, but full regulatory text (particularly Chapter 12 on Search Requests) is not easily accessible online.
