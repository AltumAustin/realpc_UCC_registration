## MARYLAND UCC FILING AND SEARCH ACCESS REQUIREMENTS - RESEARCH FINDINGS

### 1. FILING OFFICE
**Agency:** Maryland Department of Assessments and Taxation (SDAT)
- **Note:** Unlike many states, Maryland does NOT use the Secretary of State for UCC filings
- **Authority:** Since July 1, 2001, under Revised Article 9 of the Uniform Commercial Code, SDAT serves as the central filing office for public notices of secured transactions
- **Exceptions:** Mortgages and fixtures are filed with County Clerks; all other collateral types (including transmitting utilities and manufactured homes) file with SDAT

**Confidence Level:** HIGH ✓

---

### 2. AUTHORIZATION MODEL
**Model Type:** Hybrid - Public Portal with Optional Subscription

**Public Access:**
- **Open Access:** Anyone can file UCC forms online through the eGov Maryland system without a subscription
- **Free Searches:** Basic UCC searches are available for free through the public portal
- **No Eligibility Restrictions:** No background checks, bonding, or special qualifications required for basic access

**Optional Subscription Program:**
- **Provider:** Tyler Maryland (division of NICUSA, Inc.) - Maryland's eGovernment service provider
- **Annual Fee:** $95.00
- **Benefits:** Monthly consolidated billing for all transactions, eliminates need to enter payment information for each transaction, 24/7 access to all Maryland.gov subscriber services
- **Registration:** Can register online, but requires completed subscriber agreement via fax (800-861-0218) or email (mdsubscriptions@tylertech.com)

**Confidence Level:** HIGH ✓

---

### 3. REQUIREMENTS

**A. Forms:**
- **Recommended:** Standard National UCC Financing Statement forms (UCC-1, UCC-3, UCC-5)
- **Not Required:** SDAT does not mandate specific forms but strongly recommends national standard forms
- **Available Forms:** UCC-1 (initial filing), UCC-3 (amendments), UCC-5 (information statements)

**B. Fees:**
- **Standard UCC Filings:**
  - 1-8 pages: $25 (paper or electronic)
  - 9+ pages: $75
  - Note: Earlier source cited $20 for electronic filings; most recent sources indicate $25
- **Convenience Fee:** $4.50 per transaction (added December 1, 2024) for electronic payment methods (credit card/ACH)
- **Exemption:** Paper checks are not subject to convenience fee
- **Subscription Fee:** $95.00 annual fee for Tyler Maryland subscriber account (optional)

**C. Eligibility:**
- **No Restrictions:** Anyone can file UCC forms
- **No Background Checks Required**
- **No Special Qualifications Required**
- **Authorization Note:** Filings must be authorized by the debtor but do not require physical signatures on the UCC-1 form itself (attachments may require debtor signature)

**D. Required Documentation:**
- **Subscriber Account:** Completed subscriber registration agreement (if using subscription billing)
- **Filing Information:** Debtor's exact legal name (as registered with SDAT for organizations), secured party name and address, collateral description
- **Mandatory Fields:** Send Acknowledgment To (Name), Contact Email Address (verified), Address Line 1, City/Province/Region, State, Zip/Postal Code, Country

**E. Background Checks:**
- **Not Required:** No background checks for filing or searching UCC records

**F. Technical Requirements:**
- **System Availability:** 24/7 online access
- **Payment Methods:** Credit card, ACH, or paper check (for non-subscribers)
- **Browser:** Standard web browser access to egov.maryland.gov
- **User Guide:** Comprehensive PDF user guide available at https://egov.maryland.gov/SDAT/UCCFiling/UCCUserGuide.pdf

**G. Training:**
- **Not Required:** No mandatory training
- **Resources Available:** 
  - Online user guide with line-by-line instructions
  - Page-by-page instructions within the application (Instructions button)
  - Progress indicators showing position in filing process
  - Filing instructions available at dat.maryland.gov

**H. Bonding:**
- **Not Required:** No bonding requirements identified in search results

**Confidence Level:** HIGH ✓

## SUMMARY

Maryland operates a **hybrid public access model** for UCC filings and searches. The system is notably **open and accessible** with:

✓ **No barriers to entry** - anyone can file or search without special authorization
✓ **Free basic searches** available to the public
✓ **Optional subscription** for convenience and consolidated billing
✓ **No background checks, bonding, or training requirements**
✓ **24/7 online access** with immediate filing capability
✓ **Reasonable fees** ($25 for standard filings, free basic searches, $2 certified searches)
✓ **Multiple access methods** (online, mail, in-person)

The SDAT (not Secretary of State) serves as the central filing office, and Tyler Maryland manages the eGovernment portal. The system is designed for ease of use with comprehensive online guides and support.
