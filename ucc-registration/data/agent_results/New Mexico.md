### 2. AUTHORIZATION MODEL
**Model Type:** Open Access with Authorization Requirements

**Key Details:**
- **Public Filing Access:** Anyone can create an account and file UCC documents online
- **Authorization Required:** Financing statements must be authorized by the debtor to be legally effective
- **Debtor Authorization Methods:**
  - In an authenticated record
  - By authenticating or becoming bound as a debtor by a security agreement
  - By acquiring collateral
- **Search Access:** Completely open to the public with no registration required
- **No Professional Licensing:** No evidence of special licensing, certification, or registration requirements for professional UCC searchers or filing agents

**Important Note:** While anyone can file, unauthorized filings may be ineffective and subject to civil or criminal sanctions.

**Confidence Level:** HIGH ✓

### 3. REQUIREMENTS

#### A. Forms Required
- All filings must use forms prescribed by the Secretary of State
- Available forms include:
  - UCC1 (Initial Financing Statement)
  - UCC3 (Amendment, Termination, Assignment, Continuation)
  - UCC5 (Information Statement)
  - UCC11 (Information Request for certified copies)
  - EFS (Agricultural Lien Filings)

**Confidence Level:** HIGH ✓

#### B. Filing Fees (As of 2024-2026)

**Standard Written Filings:**
- $20.00 for records of 1-3 pages
- $40.00 for records of 4-25 pages
- Additional pages beyond 25: fee structure not specified in search results

**Electronic Filings:**
- Lower fees than written filings (statute mandates lower fees as incentive)
- Specific electronic filing amounts: Not disclosed in search results

**Special Filings:**
- Transmitting utility filings: Standard fee + $100.00
- Manufactured-home transactions: Standard fee + $100.00
- UCC3 Amendment: $20.00

**Information Requests (UCC11):**
- Specific fees not disclosed in search results
- Free unlimited searches via online portal
- Fees apply for certified copies (amount not specified)

**Important Notes:**
- All UCC filing fees are nonrefundable (applies to both accepted and rejected filings)
- Number of debtors indexed does not affect fee
- Prepaid accounts are no longer available

**Confidence Level:** MEDIUM - Basic fees confirmed, but complete electronic filing and information request fee schedule not fully disclosed

1. **Open Access Model:** New Mexico operates an open-access UCC system with no special requirements for searchers or filing agents
2. **Online-Only Filing:** As of March 18, 2024, all UCC filings must be submitted online

4. **No Professional Requirements:** No licensing, bonding, insurance, training, or background checks required
